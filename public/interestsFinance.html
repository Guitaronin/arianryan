Finance

For most of my adult life I have completely shunned money and material things. I've come to see this as an error in judgment. One does not need to be consumed by money or material things to look out for their own best interest financially. I'm still not totally in favor of capitalism, and I definitely think of banks and financial institutions as the scourge of humanity. But, regardless of how I feel about it, this is the system we've got.

As I've come to learn more about loans, debt management, credit scores, etc., I've been amazed at how many of the "rules" and "best practices" are totally counter-intuitive. In the very near future I'll be publishing content about best practices, effective debt management strategies, and some of the discoveries that I've found surprising.

If there's a specific question that you have, or something you'd like to know more about, shoot me a message. I'm not an expert, but I may be able to point out some helpful resources.
